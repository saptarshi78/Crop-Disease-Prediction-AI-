Grape - Black Rot

About the Disease

Black rot is caused by the fungus Guignardia bidwellii. It affects grapevines, causing dark lesions on leaves, stems, and fruit.

Symptoms

Symptoms include black, sunken lesions on fruit and leaves, leading to shriveling and premature drop.

Solutions

To control black rot:

  • Apply fungicides at key growth stages.
  • Practice crop rotation and vineyard sanitation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant material to reduce spore sources.
