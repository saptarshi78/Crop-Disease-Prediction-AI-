Corn (Maize) - Northern Leaf Blight

About the Disease

Northern leaf blight is caused by the fungus Exserohilum turcicum, characterized by long, grayish-green lesions on leaves.

Symptoms

Symptoms include elongated, grayish lesions on leaves, leading to reduced yield and quality.

Solutions

To control northern leaf blight:

  • Plant resistant corn hybrids.
  • Apply fungicides if conditions are favorable for disease development.
  • Practice crop rotation and remove infected debris.
